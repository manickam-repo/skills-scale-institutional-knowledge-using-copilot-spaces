# OctoAcme Project Management Docs — Overview & Index

## Summary of Project Management Processes

OctoAcme uses a lightweight, iterative, and transparent project management model emphasizing clear ownership, customer value, and continuous improvement. The process is organized into the following stages:

### **Initiation**
Validate business need, align stakeholders, and define measurable outcomes before moving forward. Deliverables include a Project One-pager with problem statement, goals, success metrics, stakeholders, and initial resource estimates.

### **Planning**
Break down approved work into shippable increments, estimate scope, identify dependencies and risks, and create a prioritized backlog with clear acceptance criteria and Definition of Done.

### **Execution & Tracking**
Manage day-to-day delivery through daily standups, weekly syncs, and sprint/iteration reviews. Track progress using a project board, enforce quality through testing and CI/CD, and escalate blockers through defined escalation paths.

### **Risk Management & Communication**
Maintain a risk register, identify and monitor dependencies, provide regular stakeholder updates, and establish clear communication cadences and escalation protocols.

### **Release & Deployment**
Standardize release processes with pre-release checklists, deployment verification, rollback procedures, and release notes. Minimize production risk through staging validation and smoke testing.

### **Retrospective & Continuous Improvement**
Capture learnings after each sprint, release, or milestone. Convert insights into actionable improvements tracked in the backlog with clear owners and success criteria.

### **Roles & Personas**
Define core roles—Project Manager, Product Manager, Developers, QA/Testing—with clear responsibilities, communication patterns, and goals aligned to project success.

## Key Principles

- **Customer-first**: Prioritize customer value and usability in all decisions
- **Iterative delivery**: Deliver small, testable increments and gather feedback early
- **Clear ownership**: Each project has named PM and Product Manager with defined responsibilities
- **Data-informed decisions**: Measure impact and iterate based on evidence
- **Psychological safety**: Encourage feedback, learning, and transparent communication
- **Continuous improvement**: Treat retrospectives and action items as critical to process evolution
